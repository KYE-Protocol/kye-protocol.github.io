Pilot snapshots

Six anonymised pilots in flight.

Tier-1 European bank · agentic payment authority pilot

Problem: AI agents inside the bank’s commercial-banking workflow were proposing payment instructions; the existing IPG had no way to verify that each instruction was authorised by the corporate principal under a delegation chain that satisfied PSD3 SCA + DORA Art. 17 audit-trail requirements.

KYE in scope: Reference Gateway behind the bank’s existing PSP layer; KYE Payment Profile™; webhook-signed Decision Map™ per payment intent; signed evidence pack handed to internal audit on every payment.

Outcome target: close the audit cycle on agentic payments in days rather than the current weeks.

UK payment-infrastructure provider · checkout-guard pilot

Problem: agent-backed checkout flows were arriving at the merchant gateway with no portable proof that the buying agent was authorised to make the purchase from this merchant for this basket under this customer’s limits.

KYE in scope: KYE Checkout Guard™ (planned commercial app) backed by the reference Gateway; basket-hash schema; per-checkout signed Decision Map™; merchant-side approval workflow.

Outcome target: agent-backed disputes resolved in under 24 hours via signed evidence pack instead of multi-week chargeback cycles.

UAE regulatory-sandbox programme · open-finance authority harness

Problem: a national open-finance sandbox needed a single authority-evidence format that all participants and the supervisor could verify offline with public keys.

KYE in scope: KYE Open Finance Authority Gateway™ (planned); UAE Open Finance Sandbox Connector™; Regulatory Sandbox Evidence Harness™; signed conformance reports per participant.

Outcome target: supervisor accepts a single verifiable evidence-pack format; all sandbox participants demonstrate conformance against the same fixture set.

Top-five US healthtech · healthcare break-glass authority pilot

Problem: emergency-access (HIPAA §164.308 break-glass) authority was being granted via ad-hoc tickets; no provable, replayable record of who authorised what under which clinical context.

KYE in scope: KYE Healthcare Profile™ + healthcare break-glass categories vocabulary; signed break-glass-grant + compromise-cascade fixtures; internal audit dashboard backed by KYE Self-Audit™ attestations.

Outcome target: every break-glass event has a signed, replayable proof bundle within 60 seconds of the grant.

UK Magic-Circle law firm · matter-authority + legal-hold pilot

Problem: external counsel + in-house legal teams were exchanging matter authority + privilege-classification + legal-hold instructions across systems with no portable signed record. Litigation hold preservation was being challenged on integrity grounds.

KYE in scope: KYE Legal Profile™ — KYE Contract Authority Gateway™, Matter Authority Workspace™, Legal Hold & Preservation™ apps; signature-authority connectors for the firm’s e-signature stack.

Outcome target: a single legal-evidence pack format admissible across counsel, court, and audit; chain-of-custody verified offline with public keys.

UK pension administrator · member-authority + adviser-authority pilot

Problem: regulated advisers acting on member-pension data needed a portable, FCA-friendly proof of consent + authority + scope, not a vendor-locked dashboard claim.

KYE in scope: KYE Pension Authority Connector™ + KYE Pension Sandbox Harness™; signed find / view authority payloads; adviser authority dashboard.

Outcome target: regulator accepts a single per-adviser evidence-pack format across all administrators they oversee.

Engagement shape

What a pilot looks like.

  • MOU first. Pilots start under a Memorandum of Understanding that pins the conformance suite version, the profile subset in scope, the deployment topology, the break-glass drill cadence, and the v1.1 roadmap items the Customer cares about.
  • Reference Gateway in dev / pilot environment. The open-source reference Gateway runs alongside the Customer’s existing stack — not in front of the production payment rail or audit ledger on day one.
  • Sector profile in scope. The pilot uses the relevant Connector Profile™ (Payment, Open Finance, Health, Pension, Legal) plus the Core profile.
  • Signed evidence on day one. Every authorisation produces a Decision Map™ + evidence pack from the first transaction; auditors and supervisors can verify offline immediately.
  • Quarterly drill. Tier-1 Partner Customers run a joint break-glass drill every quarter and a joint failover drill annually, per private/runbooks/customer-sla.md §8.
